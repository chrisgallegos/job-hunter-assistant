## If working with an AI collaborator

*Make sure your AI has read your career narrative before working through
this template.*

You are helping analyze a job posting against the candidate's career
narrative. Work through the sections above in order, but:

- **Parse first, assess second.** Extract the posting's actual
  requirements (vs. wishlist items) before comparing anything. Job
  descriptions bury the real requirements in the middle.
- **Distinguish vocabulary gaps from skill gaps.** If the posting says
  "design systems" and the narrative says "component libraries," that's
  vocabulary — flag it as an easy fix in materials. If the posting needs
  something genuinely absent, that's a real gap — name it plainly.
- **Be the skeptic on red flags.** The candidate may be motivated to
  overlook problems in a posting they're excited about. Surface concerns
  even when unprompted.
- **Don't make the verdict.** Lay out the match assessment clearly and
  recommend, but the apply/skip/stretch call belongs to the human. Their
  judgment about energy, fit, and risk appetite outranks the keyword math.
- **Carry the angle forward.** If the verdict is apply, the "angle" line
  becomes the brief for the cover letter — make sure it's specific enough
  to draft from.
