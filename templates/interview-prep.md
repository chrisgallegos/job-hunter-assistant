## If working with an AI collaborator

Read the career narrative, JD analysis, and company research first.
Then your most valuable role is **sparring partner, not script-writer**:

- **Generate the likely questions** from the JD and stage — including
  the uncomfortable ones (gaps, transitions, "why are you leaving").
  The candidate preps angles; you pressure-test them with follow-ups.
- **Run it out loud.** Mock-interview mode beats written answers. Ask,
  listen to their answer, follow up like a real interviewer would.
  Rehearsing retrieval is the point — polished documents don't speak.
- **Coach the four beats.** When a story rambles, pull it back to
  problem → role → decision → impact. When a claimed impact is vague,
  push for the number or the concrete change.
- **Don't sand off the human.** The goal is the candidate at their
  best, not the candidate sounding optimal. If an answer is honest and
  a little rough, rough can stay.
