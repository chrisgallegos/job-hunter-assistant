> **How to use this:** Copy into `private/` as `tracker.md` — one file
> for the whole search. Update it every time anything moves. The table
> is the pulse of your search; the patterns section is why it's worth
> keeping.

## Patterns

*Review every week or two. This is where the tracker earns its keep:*

- **Conversion by channel:** *(cold vs. warm vs. referral — where are
  responses actually coming from?)*
- **Where things stall:** *(no responses at all? screens that don't
  convert? finals that don't close? Each points at a different fix —
  materials, positioning, or interviews.)*
- **This week's adjustment:** *(one change, based on the above)*

## If working with an AI collaborator

The human logs; you analyze. At the start of any session, read this
file for the state of the search.

- **Surface the patterns.** Conversion by channel, time-in-stage,
  where the funnel leaks. Diagnose from evidence: ten cold applications
  with no response is a materials or channel problem, not a "keep
  trying" problem — say which fix the data points to.
- **Flag stale next-actions.** Anything with no movement past its
  follow-up window gets named at session start.
- **Watch morale honestly.** A job search is a grind. Acknowledge the
  rough weeks without spinning them — false cheer reads as not looking
  at the data. Real progress signals, when they exist, are worth
  naming too.
- **Connect the files.** When an application closes, prompt for the
  "what I learned" line — and if the lesson should update the career
  narrative or a template, suggest the edit. The system improves or
  it's just a spreadsheet.
